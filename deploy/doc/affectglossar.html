Affect Glossary

  
  • Affect. General term for all types of feelings.

  • AffectScript. A sequence of Appraisal Tag and Dialog Act Tag items. AffectScripts are in the AML format.

  • Appraisal Tag. A tag that represents a specific appraisal for a situation.

  • AML. Stands for Affect Modeling Language. AML is a XML sublanguage. All data and files needed and produced by ChracterBuilder and ALMA relies on this format.

  • Character. A character in ALMA represents an entity or an individual that can have affect. A character has individual personality traits.

  • Dialog Act Tag. A tag that represents the intentional content of an utterance.

  • Emotion. An emotion reflects short-term affect, which is usually elicited by a specific event, action or object. Emotions usually decay and disappear of the individual's focus. ALMA uses the 24 OCC emotion types.

  • Mood. Mood reflects medium-term affect, which is generally not related with a concrete event, action or object. Mood is a longer lasting stable affective state that influences human's cognitive functions. ALMA uses the PAD mood theory to compute a character's default mood and changes in mood according to elicited emotions.

  • Personality. Personality reflects long-term affect and describes individual differences in mental characteristics. ALMA uses the BigFive model of personality.

  • PAD Mood. Mood is described with the traits Pleasure, Arousal, and Dominance. Hence the name PAD mood. The 3 traits form a 3-dimensional mood space with 8 octants. An octant stands for a discrete mood: +P+A+D is exuberant, -P+A-D is bored, +P+A-D is dependent, -P-A+D is disdainful, +P-A+D is relaxed, -P+A-D is anxious, +P-A-D is docile, and -P+A+D is hostile.
